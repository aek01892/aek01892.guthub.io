Eagle Tavern Girl Scout Gold Award

Ambassador Girl Scout: Addie Kelly

Watkinsville, Georgia

" In the summer of my final year of High School I embarked on the huge task to receive my Girl Scout Gold Award; the highest award a scout like myself can earn. I looked around my community and found that the history that surrounded it was underappreciated. And so with the help from my friends, family, scouts and of course my community I created a video tour guide of Watkinsville's own Eagle Tavern. " -Addie Kelly

I started by meeting with the Tourism Director of the Watkinsville Visitor Center, Alex Pershcka who approved of the plan. Later I raised over $800 dollars from company sponsors in order to film. In order to write a script for the video I met with one of the writers for GPB ( Georgia Public Broadcasting ) With a scirpt, videogrpaher, and cast at hand we met at the Eagle Tavern at 6 am to 4 pm in order to film the tour guide of every single room in the buidling. With a lot of hard work and plenty of re-runs I had made a video tour guide of historic Eagle Tavern for the members of my community to educate and enjoy.

Girl Scouting began on March 12, 1912. Juliette "Daisy" Gordon Low organized Girl Guide troop meetings of 18 girls in Savannah, Georgia. Within months, members were hiking through the woods, playing basketball and going on camping trips. Today there are over 10 million members over 146 countries.
